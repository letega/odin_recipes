Chicken spaghetti

Chicken spaghetti ready to be eaten!

If you are looking for a way to jazz up your next spaghetti dinner, OR possibly looking for a creative way to use up some leftover or rotisserie chicken, you have come to the right place. This creamy, cheesy, chicken spaghetti is so incredibly flavorful, make-ahead and freezer-friendly, and your family will love it. Take a look at how to make it, read through my pro tips, storage information, and more below.

  • 1 (12 ounce) package angel hair pasta
  • 2 cups chopped cooked chicken breast
  • 1 (10.75 ounce) can condensed cream of chicken soup
  • 1 (10 ounce) can diced tomatoes with green chile peppers
  • ¾ (8 ounce) package processed cheese, cubed
  • salt and ground pepper
  • ½ (4 ounce) jar sliced mushrooms, drained

How to make chicken spaghetti easily!

  1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Drain pasta and return it to the pot
  2. Stirring occasionally, until tender yet firm to the bite, 4 to 5 minutes
  3. Cook angel hair pasta in the boiling water
  4. Add chopped chicken, condensed soup, diced tomatoes with chile peppers, processed cheese, mushrooms, salt, and pepper
  5. Cook and stir over low heat until cheese is melted and mixture is heated through, about 5 minutes
